The Man in the Middle I am a solitary man without a place where I can find peace. I am not alone. In fact, I am involved in so many different relationships that none of them are permanent. I always stand in a difficult place. I am kept at arms length and viewed as a dangerous element by both sides. I am left standing outside the collectve, seen on the one hand as a fugitive and on the other as an intruder. As a result of being observed intently by others, however, I have come to observe others with great intensity. The less I am one of them and the more apart from everyone else I am, the better I can see them. The dynamics of distance have made me what I am.
